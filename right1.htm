(January 17th, 2023)
To the surprise of no one, I suddenly left Discord again. I never really liked it. I'm sorry I keep doing this.

It killed forums, but wasn't a replacement for them. I never really liked IRC much either. The vibe and pace of conversation is far different, more involved- yet more ephemeral. Some people's attitudes rub me more the wrong way in such a scenario, too, and I'm sure it's the same of me to others.

I would like to be somewhat involved with the community again, like I was over ten years ago- and yet, the most "supported" Re-Volt forum, "The Re-Volt Hideout", is practically dead-in-the-water. The new scene lives and thrives on Discord.

I am pulled back to Discord for the community. However, I am pulled away from Discord via its paradigm, its vibe. It lacks the roominess or the touch-and-go of a moderately active forum. This isn't specifically about Re-Volt, nor do I blame any one person for this. It's a systemic problem developed by this means of communication- by the culture surrounding it. It brings out things in people I find irritating.

I don't exactly know what to do. I feel like all I need is a friend or two with their eyes on the community- to be my filter.
